Xuhu Wan

Associate Professor working at the intersection of economic theory, quantitative finance, and data science.

My research focuses on information design, dynamic contracting, and machine-learning approaches to asset pricing. Current interests include AI-driven game theory, contracting under new frictions, and how information disclosure shapes financial markets.

Effort-Induced Impatience and Optimal Compensation Design

January 2024 · 42 pages · SSRN

A continuous-time principal-agent model in which the agent's impatience rises endogenously with effort. The optimal payment threshold is non-monotonic in impatience — backloaded at low risk, frontloaded above a critical level — with closed-form implementations using debt, credit lines, and inside equity. § Empirical Demonstration

Machine Learning in Practice

Two systematic strategies running on live market data, built to demonstrate how the machine-learning and asset-pricing methods studied in my research perform out-of-sample.

Both strategies were backtested over 20 years of historical data and have been live-traded since March 2024. The charts below show actual live-trading NAV, not backtest. Results are presented as empirical illustrations of academic methods, not as an investment offering.
