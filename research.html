Effort-Induced Impatience and Optimal Compensation Design

January 2024 · 42 pages · Single-authored · SSRN

This paper develops a continuous-time principal-agent model in which the agent's impatience rises endogenously with effort. We derive explicit value functions and characterize optimal contracts. A central finding is that the payment threshold is non-monotonic in the agent's impatience level: when asset risk is low, the principal initially backloads payments to stretch incentives, but beyond a critical impatience level, the contract shifts toward frontloading, driven by two forces: stronger discounting of future payment and the rising cost of sustaining incentive under greater impatience. Implementing the contract with debt, credit lines, and inside equity yields closed-form valuations, showing that equity value shifts from concave to convex as asset risk increases. Temporary suspension efficiently realigns incentives without termination. Finally, we provide explicit conditions for renegotiation-proof contracts, revealing how effort-induced impatience reshapes incentive structures.

Dynamic Monitoring and Information Design

Working Paper · Theory

When the principal can design both the monitoring technology and the information disclosed to the agent, optimal contracts combine a state-dependent monitoring schedule with a strategically obfuscated signal. Bridges dynamic contracting and Bayesian persuasion: the optimal disclosure rule reduces required incentives by shaping the agent's beliefs about future enforcement.

Sticky Compensation in Continuous Time

Working Paper · Theory

A continuous-time contracting framework with adjustment frictions in pay. Compensation that responds slowly to performance shocks emerges optimally when adjustment is costly or socially observable. Quantitative implications for pay-performance smoothing, severance design, and the empirical "stickiness" of executive compensation contracts.

Research Themes

Dynamic Contracting

Continuous-time principal–agent problems and optimal compensation under realistic frictions: hidden effort, screening, lump-sum payments, exit options, and adjustment costs. Recent focus: how new constraints — endogenous impatience, monitoring design, contract stickiness — reshape classical optima.

Information Design

Persuasion, signaling, and dynamic disclosure in strategic environments. Optimal communication policies under commitment, applications to managerial communication and earnings guidance, and the integration of information design with classical contracting theory.

Asset Pricing & Machine Learning

Stochastic discount factors, factor models, and deep-learning approaches to cross-sectional return prediction. How regime-aware ML models compare to IPCA and traditional factor benchmarks, especially during transitions. The two strategies in the ML in Practice section serve as live empirical demonstrations.

Information & Financial Markets

How disclosure rules and news flow propagate into asset prices, volatility, and liquidity. Connecting theoretical results in information design with empirical patterns in market microstructure and event-driven returns.

AI-Driven Game Theory

When players are reinforcement learners rather than expected-utility maximizers, equilibrium concepts and welfare implications shift. Implications for market design, disclosure policy, and regulation in environments populated by algorithmic decision-makers.
